Vital City

Contact master search — how it works

A single, searchable view of everyone in Vital City's orbit. It pulls together four lists, matches the same person across them and lets you slice the result any way you like and pull out contact or email lists.

You can't break anything — explore freely. Hover your mouse over any button, column heading or tag and a little explanation pops up. Nothing here is permanent until you choose to save, and even then it's easy to undo. If you ever feel lost, click Reset all to return to the full list.

Quick start (60 seconds)

  1. Find someone: type a name, email or organization in the search box at the top. Results filter as you type.
  2. See a group: click Subscribers, Donors or Authors (the buttons under "Show:"), or click one of the big numbers across the top. Click a second one to see the overlap.
  3. Order the list: use the Sort menu (e.g. "Recently subscribed", "Recently donated", "Most engaged").
  4. Do something with the result: Export CSV (a spreadsheet), Copy emails, or Email group.
  5. View someone's details: click their name (or anywhere on their row) to open their full card — name, organization, every email, types and notes. Edit or delete right from there. Click the star ☆ to flag someone you'll want again.
  6. Share: Copy link to this view (a saved search) or open a person and hit 🔗 Copy link (that one contact's card).

What's in it

Each person is one row, built by merging four sources (matched by email, then by name):

SourceWhat it adds
Newsletter subscribers (from Ghost)The Subscriber badge + the year they joined. "Subscriber" means the free email list — not the same as a donor.
Contacts (the Google Sheet your team maintains)The type tags: VC contributor, VC advisor, journalist, academic, foundation leadership, nonprofit leadership, city gov, current nyc.gov, state gov, fed gov, judge — plus specialty areas (criminal justice, housing, transit, budget, urban planning, education, public health, economy, technology, politics & government, race & equity, culture, architecture).
Donors (from Donorbox)The $ amount badge (total given) and the date of their most recent gift.
Authors (vitalcitynyc.org)The "N pieces" badge (articles written).
Subscriber ≠ donor. Someone can be a donor without being a newsletter subscriber, and vice-versa. Turn on Donors and set Subscribers to "Not subscribers" to see donors who haven't joined the email list — good people to invite to subscribe.

How types & domains get assigned

Beyond what's in the contacts sheet, the tool fills in categories automatically:

  • Journalists are also inferred from the email someone subscribed with — if it's a known news-outlet domain (nytimes.com, wnyc.org, thecity.nyc, politico.com, etc.), they're tagged journalist.
  • Current nyc.gov flags anyone who subscribed with an active New York City email (anything ending nyc.gov — agencies, city hall, DAs). It's separate from city gov (which is the hand-maintained list and can include former officials), so after an administration change you can find who's currently inside city government.
  • Domain interests are inferred from what people have written for Vital City — an author of pieces tagged Housing gets the housing domain, Budget → budget, and so on.
  • New authors are added automatically whenever a new piece is published: on the next refresh they appear tagged author, with the domains they wrote about, and their email filled in if they're already in any source list.

The Show toggles — overlaps and exclusions

The Show: row has four chips — Subscribers · Donors · Authors · Other. Each one cycles through three states as you click it:

  • Off → Include (colored) → Exclude (turns magenta and reads "Not subscribers / Not donors / Not authors") → off again.
  • Combine them for overlaps. Subscribers + Authors (both Include) = people who are both. Donors + "Not subscribers" = donors who haven't joined the email list. Authors + "Not subscribers" = contributors who don't get the newsletter. Any mix works.
  • Other = none of the three (not a subscriber, donor or author).
  • When Donors is on, a Repeat donors (2+ gifts) checkbox appears to narrow to people who have given more than once.

Searching & filtering

  • Type / specialty (left checkboxes): tick any combination — they're OR'd together (e.g. journalist + academic shows either). The little ⓘ next to each heading explains that these are partly inferred/extrapolated, so treat them as approximate.
  • Subscribed between (left): filter to people who joined the newsletter within a date range.
  • Search box: matches name, email, institution or notes.
  • ★ Starred and Notable checkboxes (top bar) narrow to just your starred contacts, or just people flagged notable (see below).
  • 2+ newsletter emails (top bar) shows only people subscribed to the newsletter under two or more addresses (each tagged ✉ newsletter) — i.e. they're on the list more than once.
  • The banner numbers are clickable shortcuts — click "subscribers", "donors", "authors" or "other" to turn that filter on (click again to turn it off); combine several. Reset all returns to the full list.
  • A VC contributor chip shows ×N for the number of pieces and is a link — click it to open the public article catalogue filtered to that author's pieces.
  • People can have several emails — all are shown and searchable. Hover an email to get a one-click copy button; every address is also a mailto: link. When someone has more than one email, each address they're subscribed to the newsletter through gets a green ✉ newsletter tag (all of them, if they subscribe under several); the address they most recently opened the newsletter from reads ✉ newsletter · last opened (in blue). Addresses without a tag aren't newsletter subscriptions (e.g. a work or byline email). Only addresses Ghost confirms as active subscribers are tagged.

Sorting

Use the Sort menu in the toolbar to order the list:

  • Last name (the default) and First name — alphabetical. Compound surnames like "Jones Austin" stay together; middle initials and quoted nicknames are dropped; people with no name sort to the bottom.
  • Recently subscribed / Longest subscribed — by the date someone joined the newsletter (newest or oldest first). Pair Recently subscribed with the Subscribers filter for a clean "newest members" list.
  • Recently donated — by the date of each person's most recent gift. Pair it with the Donors filter for "who gave most recently".
  • Most published (VC pieces) — by how many pieces a person has written for Vital City, most prolific first. Pair it with the Authors filter to rank your most frequent contributors.
  • Most engaged — by email engagement (see below). Donation size — by lifetime total given.

You can also sort by clicking any column header (an arrow ▲/▼ shows the direction); the Sort menu stays in sync. People who lack the value you're sorting by — e.g. non-donors when sorting by gift date — fall to the bottom.

Email engagement (open rate)

Under each subscriber you'll see their average newsletter open rate (e.g. 72% opens), pulled from Mailchimp — the share of our emails that person opens. Two caveats: Apple Mail's privacy feature auto-opens messages, which can inflate the number; and a brand-new subscriber can show a high % off just a handful of emails. Sort by Most engaged to rank readers by Mailchimp's overall engagement score (which also weighs clicks and how long they've been on the list).

Notable (Wikipedia) flag

Names shown with a cerulean underline are flagged notable — they appear to have an English Wikipedia page. The match is deliberately conservative: a hit only counts when the Wikipedia summary also contains a context clue for this person (a word from their institution, a keyword for their type/specialty or "New York"), so an unrelated same-named person doesn't get tagged. Tick the Notable checkbox to see only these. It's a rough signal of public profile, not a judgment — and it's only ever checked against named, known contacts, never anonymous subscribers.

Unsubscribed (former contacts)

People who left the newsletter are kept separate and shown in red. They're hidden by default — tick Include unsubscribed to mix them in, or click the red unsubscribed banner number to see only them. The other banner counts reflect current contacts only. This list syncs automatically from Mailchimp each day, so when someone unsubscribes there they drop off the subscriber list on the next refresh. If they come back — i.e. Ghost shows them actively subscribed again — they're automatically moved back to being a current subscriber (Ghost's live status always wins over an old unsubscribe).

Getting lists out

  • Export CSV — downloads the full filtered list. Includes separate first_name and last_name columns (alongside the full name) so you can mail-merge a greeting like "Dear [first_name]," plus email, types, member/author/donor status and giving.
  • Copy emails — copies the subset's unique emails to your clipboard (comma-separated), ready to paste anywhere.
  • Email group — pick Bcc / To / Cc, then it opens your default mail app with the subset's addresses in that field. (Big lists may exceed what a mail app accepts — use Copy emails for those.)

Saving and sharing a view

  • Save current view — name any filter + sort combination (e.g. "Housing reporters '24+") and it's saved on your device, one click to reapply.
  • Copy link to this view — copies a link that reopens the exact same filters, sort and search for anyone with the passphrase. (The current view is also kept in the page address as you go, so a normal bookmark works too.)
  • Link to one person's card — open anyone (click ✎) and hit 🔗 Copy link to copy a link that opens straight to that contact's card. Handy for "here's so-and-so" in an email or Slack. The link works for anyone with the passphrase, and it carries a scrambled id rather than the person's email, so no address leaks into the URL.
  • Needs review — a guided queue of records that could use a human check: educated-guess names, missing institutions and possible duplicates. The tool already de-duplicates automatically (matching the same person across lists by email and name), so this is a short clean-up list, not a pile.
  • Merge duplicates — open Needs review → possible duplicates. Each likely-duplicate group has a ⤵ Merge these button that combines them into one record: it keeps every email (bolding the subscription one), every type and note, and the strongest status — if any copy is a subscriber, donor or author, the merged record is too. Use this when, say, confirming a guessed name accidentally creates a second copy of someone already in the list. The merge saves for everyone.

Dark mode & using it on your phone

  • Dark mode — toggle it from the 🌙 Dark mode link in the top bar; your choice is remembered.
  • On a phone, the table reflows into one tidy card per person (name, then the fields that person actually has) instead of a cramped, side-scrolling grid.
  • Install it as an app. The tool is an installable web app. On iPhone (Safari): Share → Add to Home Screen. On Android (Chrome): menu → Install app / Add to Home screen. It then opens full-screen with its own icon, like a native app, and still asks for the passphrase. It even opens (from its cached copy) when you're offline, showing the last data it loaded.

Viewing, fixing or cutting an entry

Click anyone's name — or anywhere on their row — to open their full card. (The pencil ✎ does the same thing.) From that card you can read everything and also change that person: name, institution, emails, types and specialties, add a private note, or Delete entry (the delete button lives inside the card, so it can't be hit by accident). Nothing changes until you click Save, so it's safe to open a card just to look. A gray italic name is an educated guess from the email (e.g. aaron@naparstek.com → Aaron Naparstek); the ✓ next to one confirms it's right (turns it black). Opening a card and looking around won't confirm anything — only changing the name or clicking ✓ does. If a name ever turns black by mistake, open the card and click ↩ Revert to guess to make it gray again.

  • Notes — anything you type in the note field is saved, searchable and shown as a small 📝 on the row (hover to read it).
  • Star a contact — click the ☆ at the start of any row to flag people you want to find fast; the ★ Starred filter shows just those.
  • Email this contact card — from the card, ✉ Email this card copies a tidy summary of that person (name, institution, emails, types, giving) to your clipboard so you can paste it into any email. If you have a desktop mail app, it also opens a ready-made draft.
Your edits stick automatically. Every edit, confirmation and deletion is saved to a shared store (a Google Sheet behind the scenes), not just your browser — so it persists for everyone and survives every future refresh. You no longer have to export anything. (The change shows for you immediately; it appears for everyone else on the next daily refresh.)

How the data updates — what's automatic vs. manual

The page is a snapshot rebuilt by an automatic job every day on GitHub's servers — so it stays current whether or not anyone's computer is on. Here's what each piece does:

DataUpdatesHow
Newsletter subscribersAutomatic, dailyPulled live from Ghost each morning. New sign-ups appear on the next day's refresh.
UnsubscribesAutomatic, dailyPulled live from Mailchimp. Anyone who unsubscribes there is flagged and removed from the subscriber list automatically. (Mailchimp is used only for the unsubscribe signal — Ghost still owns who's subscribed.)
Authors / contributorsAutomatic, dailyThe article catalogue is re-scraped; new bylines become VC contributor rows automatically.
Inferred types & specialtiesAutomaticRecomputed every refresh (journalist from news domains, current nyc.gov from city emails, domains from what people wrote).
Your edits / confirms / deletesAutomaticSaved to the shared store as you make them; folded in on the next daily refresh.
DonorsAutomatic, dailyPulled live from the Donorbox API — gifts, totals and last-gift dates refresh on their own.
Contacts / CRM typesManualMaintained in the team's contacts Google Sheet; refreshed when that export is updated.

Donors (Donorbox)

Donor data now syncs automatically from Donorbox every day — new gifts, updated lifetime totals, gift counts and last-gift dates flow in on the daily refresh. Nothing manual to do.

Adding people

One new person — a single new donor, author or contact — can be added right here: click + Add person (top of the list), fill in their details, tick what they are (subscriber / author / donor, with a donation total if relevant), and Save.

A few at a time — type rows in the Google Sheet's "add" tab (plain columns: name, email, institution, types, specialties, subscriber/author/donor, donation). They appear in the tool on the next refresh.

Leave those rows in the Sheet. The add tab is re-read on every refresh — it's the permanent home for hand-added people, not a one-time inbox. Deleting a row removes that person from the tool (unless they also come from a live source). Same for the overrides tab: deleting a row undoes that edit.

A whole list of new people (a big CSV) is a source update, not a one-off. Don't add them one-by-one. Hand the CSV to Claude Code and say what it is (new contacts, a donor export, a partner list); it folds the file into the right source and they all appear on the next refresh.

The mirror spreadsheet

A consolidated spreadsheet that mirrors this tool — Vital City — Network (master) — holds everyone with all fields for sorting/filtering in Sheets. (Editing that sheet doesn't feed back into the tool — use Edit here for that.)

Confidentiality

This contains subscriber, donor and contact data. The page is password-protected and the underlying data is encrypted — only people with the passphrase can read it. Don't redistribute it or the passphrase.

After you enter the passphrase once, the device remembers it so you don't retype it each visit. On a shared or public computer, click Lock / forget password on this device (top bar) to clear it.
