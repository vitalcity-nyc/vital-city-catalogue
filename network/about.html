Vital City

Contact master search — how it works

A single, searchable view of everyone in Vital City's orbit. It pulls together four lists, matches the same person across them, and lets you slice the result any way you like and pull out contact or email lists.

What's in it

Each person is one row, built by merging four sources (matched by email, then by name):

SourceWhat it adds
Newsletter subscribers (from Ghost)The Subscriber badge + the year they joined. "Subscriber" means the free email list — not the same as a donor.
Contacts (the Google Sheet your team maintains)The type tags: journalist, academic, funder, gov, judge, architect, nonprofit, contributor, advisor + specialty domains (criminal justice, housing, transit, budget, urban planning, education, public health, economy, technology, politics & government, race & equity, culture).
Donors (from Donorbox)The $ amount badge (total given) and the date of their most recent gift.
Authors (vitalcitynyc.org)The "N pieces" badge (articles written).
Subscriber ≠ donor. Someone can be a donor without being a newsletter subscriber, and vice-versa. Turn on Donors and set Subscribers to "Not subscribers" to see donors who haven't joined the email list — good people to invite to subscribe.

How types & domains get assigned

Beyond what's in the contacts sheet, the tool fills in categories automatically:

  • Journalists are also inferred from the email someone subscribed with — if it's a known news-outlet domain (nytimes.com, wnyc.org, thecity.nyc, politico.com, etc.), they're tagged journalist.
  • Current nyc.gov flags anyone who subscribed with an active New York City email (anything ending nyc.gov — agencies, city hall, DAs). It's separate from city gov (which is the hand-maintained list and can include former officials), so after an administration change you can find who's currently inside city government.
  • Domain interests are inferred from what people have written for Vital City — an author of pieces tagged Housing gets the housing domain, Budget → budget, and so on.
  • New authors are added automatically whenever a new piece is published: on the next refresh they appear tagged author, with the domains they wrote about, and their email filled in if they're already in any source list.

The Show toggles — overlaps and exclusions

The Show: row has four chips — Subscribers · Donors · Authors · Other. Each one cycles through three states as you click it:

  • Off → Include (colored) → Exclude (turns magenta and reads "Not subscribers / Not donors / Not authors") → off again.
  • Combine them for overlaps. Subscribers + Authors (both Include) = people who are both. Donors + "Not subscribers" = donors who haven't joined the email list. Authors + "Not subscribers" = contributors who don't get the newsletter. Any mix works.
  • Other = none of the three (not a subscriber, donor or author).
  • When Donors is on, a Repeat donors (2+ gifts) checkbox appears to narrow to people who have given more than once.

Searching & filtering

  • Type / specialty (left checkboxes): tick any combination — they're OR'd together (e.g. journalist + academic shows either). The little ⓘ next to each heading explains that these are partly inferred/extrapolated, so treat them as approximate.
  • Subscribed between (left): filter to people who joined the newsletter within a date range.
  • Search box: matches name, email or institution.
  • The banner numbers are clickable shortcuts — click "subscribers", "donors", "authors" or "other" to turn that filter on (click again to turn it off); combine several. Reset all returns to the full list.
  • Sort any column by clicking its header — an arrow (▲/▼) shows the direction. Last name is the default (compound surnames like "Jones Austin" stay together; middle initials and quoted nicknames are dropped); people with no last name sort to the bottom.
  • A VC contributor chip shows ×N for the number of pieces and is a link — click it to open the public article catalogue filtered to that author's pieces.
  • People can have several emails — all are shown and searchable.

Unsubscribed (former contacts)

People who left the newsletter are kept separate and shown in red. They're hidden by default — tick Include unsubscribed to mix them in, or click the red unsubscribed banner number to see only them. The other banner counts reflect current contacts only.

Getting lists out

  • Export CSV — downloads the full filtered list (name, email, types, member/author/donor status, giving).
  • Copy emails — copies the subset's unique emails to your clipboard (comma-separated), ready to paste anywhere.
  • Email group — pick Bcc / To / Cc, then it opens your default mail app with the subset's addresses in that field. (Big lists may exceed what a mail app accepts — use Copy emails for those.)

Fixing or cutting an entry

Click Edit on any row to change that person: name, institution, emails, types and specialties — or Delete entry (the delete button lives inside the edit window, so it can't be hit by accident). A gray italic name is an educated guess from the email (e.g. aaron@naparstek.com → Aaron Naparstek); the ✓ next to one confirms it's right (turns it black).

Your edits stick automatically. Every edit, confirmation and deletion is saved to a shared store (a Google Sheet behind the scenes), not just your browser — so it persists for everyone and survives every future refresh. You no longer have to export anything. (The change shows for you immediately; it appears for everyone else on the next daily refresh.)

How the data updates — what's automatic vs. manual

The page is a snapshot rebuilt by an automatic job every day on GitHub's servers — so it stays current whether or not anyone's computer is on. Here's what each piece does:

DataUpdatesHow
Newsletter subscribersAutomatic, dailyPulled live from Ghost each morning. New sign-ups appear on the next day's refresh.
Authors / contributorsAutomatic, dailyThe article catalogue is re-scraped; new bylines become VC contributor rows automatically.
Inferred types & specialtiesAutomaticRecomputed every refresh (journalist from news domains, current nyc.gov from city emails, domains from what people wrote).
Your edits / confirms / deletesAutomaticSaved to the shared store as you make them; folded in on the next daily refresh.
DonorsManualA snapshot from Donorbox — see below.
Contacts / CRM typesManualMaintained in the team's contacts Google Sheet; refreshed when that export is updated.

Refreshing donors (Donorbox)

Donor data is a manual snapshot, not live. To refresh it: in Donorbox, export the full donations CSV, then hand that file to Claude Code and ask it to refresh the donor list (it replaces donors_source.csv and republishes). New gifts won't show until that's done.

Adding a batch of new entries (a whole CSV)

The in-tool Edit is for tweaking or removing existing people one at a time. A whole new list of people is different — it's a source update, not an edit. Don't try to add them one-by-one in the tool. Instead, hand the CSV to Claude Code and say what it is (new contacts, a donor export, a partner list, etc.); it folds the file into the right source and the new people appear on the next refresh. So: small fix → use Edit here; a batch → bring the CSV to Claude.

The mirror spreadsheet

A consolidated spreadsheet that mirrors this tool — Vital City — Network (master) — holds everyone with all fields for sorting/filtering in Sheets. (Editing that sheet doesn't feed back into the tool — use Edit here for that.)

Confidentiality

This contains subscriber, donor and contact data. The page is password-protected and the underlying data is encrypted — only people with the passphrase can read it. Don't redistribute it or the passphrase.

After you enter the passphrase once, the device remembers it so you don't retype it each visit. On a shared or public computer, click Lock / forget password on this device (top bar) to clear it.
