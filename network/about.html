Vital City

Contact master search — how it works

A single, searchable view of everyone in Vital City's orbit. It pulls together four lists, matches the same person across them, and lets you slice the result any way you like and pull out contact or email lists.

What's in it

Each person is one row, built by merging four sources (matched by email, then by name):

SourceWhat it adds
Newsletter subscribers (from Ghost)The Subscriber badge + the year they joined. "Subscriber" means the free email list — not the same as a donor.
Contacts (the Google Sheet your team maintains)The type tags: journalist, academic, funder, gov, judge, architect, nonprofit, contributor, advisor + specialty domains (criminal justice, housing, transit, budget, urban planning, education, public health, economy, technology, politics & government, race & equity, culture).
Donors (from FCNY)The $ amount badge (total given) and the date of their most recent gift.
Authors (vitalcitynyc.org)The "N pieces" badge (articles written).
Subscriber ≠ donor. Someone can be a donor without being a newsletter subscriber, and vice-versa. If you filter "Donors + Non-subscribers," you're seeing donors who haven't joined the email list — good people to invite to subscribe.

How types & domains get assigned

Beyond what's in the contacts sheet, the tool fills in categories automatically:

  • Journalists are also inferred from the email someone subscribed with — if it's a known news-outlet domain (nytimes.com, wnyc.org, thecity.nyc, politico.com, etc.), they're tagged journalist.
  • Current nyc.gov flags anyone who subscribed with an active New York City email (anything ending nyc.gov — agencies, city hall, DAs). It's separate from city gov (which is the hand-maintained list and can include former officials), so after an administration change you can find who's currently inside city government.
  • Domain interests are inferred from what people have written for Vital City — an author of pieces tagged Housing gets the housing domain, Budget → budget, and so on.
  • New authors are added automatically whenever a new piece is published: on the next refresh they appear tagged author, with the domains they wrote about, and their email filled in if they're already in any source list.

Searching & filtering

  • Type / specialty (left checkboxes): tick any combination — they're OR'd together (e.g. journalist + academic shows either).
  • Subscribers / Non-subscribers, Author, Donor: each narrows further (AND'd with everything else).
  • Subscribed between (left): filter to people who joined the newsletter within a date range.
  • Search box: matches name, email or institution.
  • Click any type tag or the type×membership bars to filter by it. Clear filters resets everything.
  • The banner numbers are clickable shortcuts — click "subscribers", "donors", "authors", "other" (neither subscriber nor donor), etc. to jump to that group, sorted sensibly (donors by amount, authors by piece count, subscribers newest first).
  • Sort any column by clicking its header. Last name is the default; people with no last name sort to the bottom. A VC contributor tag shows ×N when they've written more than one piece.

Example: tick journalist, set Members → every journalist on the newsletter. Or academic + Donors only → academics who give.

Getting lists out

  • Export CSV — downloads the full filtered list (name, email, types, member/author/donor status, giving).
  • Copy emails — copies the subset's unique emails to your clipboard (comma-separated), ready to paste anywhere.
  • Email group — pick Bcc / To / Cc, then it opens your default mail app with the subset's addresses in that field. (Big lists may exceed what a mail app accepts — use Copy emails for those.)

Names & the gray ones

Most people have a confirmed (black) name. A gray italic name is an educated guess pulled from the email address (e.g. aaron@naparstek.com → Aaron Naparstek) — used only when no source gives a real name. You can:

  • Click ✎ to type the correct name.
  • Click ✓ next to a gray name to confirm the guess is right — it turns black.

Your fixes save in your browser right away. To make them permanent for everyone, click Export name fixes and send the file to whoever publishes (it gets baked into the data on the next publish). Note: these gray names are mostly email-only subscribers/donors who aren't in the contacts Sheet, so fixes here are tracked separately — they are not written back into the Google Sheet.

Keeping it current

The page is a snapshot that's rebuilt when someone publishes. It doesn't read the sources live.

  • Contacts & categories are maintained by the team in the Google Sheet vital-city-contacts-master. Edit there freely.
  • Subscribers come from a Ghost export; donors from an FCNY export.
  • To push everything live, a maintainer refreshes those source files and runs the one-command publish (see the maintenance playbook). Changes appear in about a minute.

Confidentiality

This contains subscriber, donor and contact data. The page is password-protected and the underlying data is encrypted — only people with the passphrase can read it. Don't redistribute it or the passphrase.

After you enter the passphrase once, the device remembers it so you don't retype it each visit. On a shared or public computer, click Lock / forget password on this device (top bar) to clear it.
